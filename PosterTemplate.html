How to copy this template
Go back to the home page (top right: 2020 Molecular & Integrative Phy etc.). In the top right corner, click the create page button (paper with a plus), it will take you to a page creation window. In that window, name your page your name, and in the template drop-down select the PosterTemplate Option. <Key> For location select Put page under 2020 Molecular & Integrative Physiology Symposium. <Key>. After that, hit create and you are golden. Once you have created your page, delete all the how-to-copy this template text.

General Advice
I put dividing lines in this template to help separate the sections. You have a header section, a picture section ( this one), and a description section. The horizontal lines should help the flow and to organize your thoughts. If they need to be deleted for some reason, simply click the line they are on and hit backspace. If you happen to put your website in the wrong location, it is not a big deal, it will exist as a subpage to one of the existing ones and can be found and moved in SiteMap. Click the 3 gray stacked bar icon in the top left corner and it will display all pages. Go to the misplaced page, click settings, then move page, and finally select 2020 Molecular (the lower one, there are two but it is the lower one you want) as the move location ... which will place it in the correct spot.  You can delete this text after reading it.

Adding an Image
Accepted File formats: .jpg, .jpeg, .gif, .png
Put your picture below this text. To upload an image, click insert (top menu) image, upload image (select the image saved to your local machine), and then hit ok. Do not mess with the link or remove it. It will then give you options for formatting and putting text on the image. When you do click 100% on the bottom photo bar to have it scale to the page. It will scale to about 915px across and 600px down on a normal window. From some basic testing, I recommend pixel dimensions somewhere around 1500 by 1500. There is a decent amount of give in this, but if don't go above 3000px by 4000px. When clicked the image will open and you can zoom in on it, so the resolution of the photo matters. Keep this in mind because if your original photo has too big of dimensions it will zoom in way too far but if it has too small dimensions it will have to stretch which will make it blurry. Generally, it is safer to have a  slightly bigger picture that scales down to maintain pixel density. Save your changes by hitting the save button. Click your image to make sure it opens toa new tab that you can zoom in on. If it doesn't zoom or appear how you like, we may have to resize the image using an outside tool. Remove this text once everything works.
